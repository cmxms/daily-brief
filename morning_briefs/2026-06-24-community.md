## 🧭 Regime read (generalized)
Dealer gamma is **negative** on both QQQ and SPY (spot well below the zero-gamma flip: QQQ 714 vs flip 733, SPY 734 vs flip 746). In negative gamma, dealer hedging **amplifies** moves instead of dampening them — that means trend/momentum behavior, with selloffs that accelerate and snap-back rallies (like this morning's) that are just as sharp and just as quick to reverse.

Translation: this is a **trend/volatility tape, not a calm mean-reversion one.** Fading extremes and knife-catching is where people get hurt right now — a green open can flip hard, especially with a binary chip earnings print tonight and VIX backwardation flagging near-term stress. Let levels prove themselves before trusting them; the bounce needs to hold, not just appear.
